The Craft

From Ocean to
your plate

Every piece of sushi at Purple Dragon follows a rigorous journey — a process refined over generations.

01

Sourcing & Selection

Before dawn at Tokyo's Toyosu Market, our buyer hand-selects each fish. Bluefin tuna from Hokkaido, uni from Muroran, kampachi from Kyushu — only the top 2% makes the cut. Every specimen is evaluated for fat content, texture, and freshness.

02

Shari — The Rice

Our shari is a 40-year recipe. Koshihikari rice from Niigata, cooked in iron pots, seasoned with a blend of Akazu red vinegar, sea salt, and kombu dashi. Each grain must be glossy, firm outside, soft within — seasoned at body temperature, never refrigerated.

03

Nikiri & Aging

Tuna is dry-aged in our dedicated chamber for 24–72 hours. This intensifies umami and deepens color. Each fish receives a different aging profile — ōtoro at 48 hours, akami at 24. The nikiri sauce is our own blend of soy, mirin, and sake, reduced over 3 days.

04

Cutting & Formation

With a Yanagiba knife sharpened to a mirror edge, Chef Tanaka cuts each piece at a precise angle determined by the fish's fat line and muscle structure. The nigiri is formed in one fluid motion — rice compressed in 3 seconds, fish draped with a single stroke. No squeezing, no hesitation.

05

The Moment of Serving

Sushi is alive — it deteriorates the moment it leaves the chef's hand. That's why each piece is placed directly before you, one at a time. The timing between courses, the temperature, the wasabi placement under the fish — everything is calculated for peak flavor at the exact second you eat.
